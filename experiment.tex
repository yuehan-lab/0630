\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}

\subsection{Data Collection}
\label{sec:data}

\noindent\textbf{Human sessions.}
We recruited 52 participants (age 19--41, 31 male, 21 female) across three naturalistic
interaction tasks performed while holding the phone in their preferred grip:
(T1)~social-media browsing (scrolling, tapping, entering search queries),
(T2)~mobile banking form filling (entering account numbers, amounts, confirming transfers),
and (T3)~prompted UI tapping (reacting to server-selected challenge buttons at random times).%
Each session lasted 30\,s; each participant completed 15 sessions per task
(2{,}340 human sessions total; mean 47 touch events per session).%
Sessions were collected across 18 Android device models spanning four families:
Pixel (6, 7, 8, 8a), Galaxy (S22, S23, S24, A54, A34), OnePlus (11, 12, Nord 3, CE 3),
Xiaomi (12, 13, 13 Pro, Redmi Note 12, Redmi Note 13).%
All data was collected with IRB approval; participants provided written informed consent.%

\noindent\textbf{Agent sessions.}
Three frameworks were used to generate agent sessions executing identical UI workflows:
AutoGLM~\cite{autoglm2024}, AppAgent~\cite{appagent2024}, and custom ADB scripts.%
For each framework we generated sessions under three adversary levels:
A0 (default, phone on flat surface), A1 (ERM vibration motor, 13\,mm, chassis-attached,
triggered synchronously with each injection), and A2 (touch timestamps resampled to
match the lognormal distribution of our human cohort).%
This produced 1{,}080 agent sessions (360 per framework × 3 adversary levels; 120 per
adversary level per framework).%

\noindent\textbf{Active-mode and overlay sessions.}
A dedicated split of 600 human sessions and 300 agent sessions included a server-selected
challenge event $t^*$.%
Overlay agent sessions (300 total) featured a legitimate user physically holding and
interacting with the phone while an ADB host injected a single fraudulent touch at $t^*$
(payment-confirmation scenario).%

\subsection{Baselines}
\label{sec:baselines}

We compare PCCN against four baselines:
\textbf{BeCAPTCHA-Accel}~\cite{becaptcha2021} (accelerometer-only binary classifier,
retrained on our dataset);
\textbf{TToS-Features}~\cite{ttos2026} (kinematic feature set from Turing Test on Screen,
gradient-boosted tree);
\textbf{MiniRocket}~\cite{minirocket2021} (state-of-the-art time-series classifier on
raw accelerometer);
\textbf{PCCN-Passive} (our passive head only, as an ablation).%

\subsection{Metrics}
\label{sec:metrics}

We report accuracy (ACC), macro F1, AUC-ROC, and equal error rate (EER).%
EER is the operating point at which FPR equals FNR.%
All results use a 5-fold cross-validation with a zero-overlap device-model split:
no device model in any test fold appears in the training fold.%

\subsection{Main Classification Results}
\label{sec:main}

Table~\ref{tab:main} reports passive-mode and active-mode results under the A0
adversary.%
PCCN-Full achieves 97.3\% ACC and 1.8\% EER in active mode, outperforming all baselines
by at least 8.9 pp in ACC.%
The kinematic baseline (TToS-Features) achieves 88.4\% under A0 but collapses to 51.2\%
under A2 timing mimicry (Section~\ref{sec:adversarial}), confirming the fundamental
limitation of statistics-based detection.%

\subsection{Physical Causal Score Analysis}
\label{sec:pcsanalysis}

Fig.~\ref{fig:pcs} shows the distribution of per-session mean PCS ($\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$)
and per-event PCS at the challenged position $t^*$, for all three session classes.%

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_pcs}
  \caption{Physical Causal Score distributions. Left: session-level $\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$
  clearly separates human from autonomous agent sessions; overlay agent sessions show
  human-like $\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$ because most touches are genuine. Right: PCS at the
  challenged position $t^*$ separates human touches from overlay injections,
  enabling event-level localization.}
  \label{fig:pcs}
\end{figure}

The left panel confirms that $\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$ alone strongly separates human
(median 0.81) from autonomous agent (median 0.08) sessions.%
Overlay agent sessions show human-like $\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$ (median 0.74),
explaining why passive mode cannot detect them---the session-level physical signal
is dominated by genuine human touches.%
The right panel shows that PCS at $t^*$ cleanly separates real human challenge responses
(median 0.79) from overlay injections (median 0.07), validating that event-level
localization via OAL is necessary and effective.%

\subsection{Cross-Device Generalization}
\label{sec:crossdevice}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_crossdevice}
  \caption{Cross-device accuracy under zero-overlap device-model split.
  PCCN-Full exceeds 95\% on all four device families, demonstrating
  device-agnostic physical causality detection.}
  \label{fig:crossdevice}
\end{figure}

PCCN-Full achieves above 95\% accuracy across all four device families
(Table data in Fig.~\ref{fig:crossdevice}).%
The smallest margin (95.4\% on Xiaomi) is attributable to higher baseline accelerometer
noise floor; per-session z-score normalization largely compensates.%
All baselines degrade by 5--8 pp on Xiaomi compared to Pixel, confirming that
PCCN's physics-grounded design generalizes more robustly than statistical baselines.%

\subsection{Adversarial Robustness}
\label{sec:adversarial}

\begin{table}[t]
\centering
\caption{Active-mode accuracy (\%) under adversary levels A0--A2.
Bold: best per adversary level. PCCN-Full is the only method that maintains
$>$94\% across all three levels.}
\label{tab:adversarial}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Method & A0 (default) & A1 (vibration motor) & A2 (timing mimicry) \\
\midrule
BeCAPTCHA-Accel  & 84.7$\pm$2.1 & 76.2$\pm$2.8 & 83.1$\pm$2.3 \\
TToS-Features    & 88.4$\pm$1.7 & 71.3$\pm$3.1 & 51.2$\pm$3.4 \\
MiniRocket        & 87.6$\pm$1.9 & 79.4$\pm$2.6 & 86.8$\pm$2.2 \\
PCCN-Passive     & 93.6$\pm$1.3 & 89.1$\pm$1.9 & 93.4$\pm$1.4 \\
PCCN-Full (ours) & \textbf{97.3}$\pm$\textbf{0.8} & \textbf{94.8}$\pm$\textbf{1.2} & \textbf{96.9}$\pm$\textbf{0.9} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent\textbf{A1 (vibration motor).}
PCCN-Full drops only 2.5 pp (to 94.8\%) against A1 adversaries,
while baselines drop 7--17 pp.%
ISPM is the key: after training, the prototype bank assigns A1 motor signals to
$\mathbf{p}_2$ (narrowband) with 89.3\% assignment accuracy, and the prototype
feature vector correctly signals ``motor, not finger'' to the classification head.%
TToS-Features drops 17.1 pp because it has no mechanism to distinguish vibration
motor signals from genuine impulses.%

\noindent\textbf{A2 (timing mimicry).}
Timing mimicry reduces TToS-Features to 51.2\% (near chance for 2-class),
confirming the fatal weakness of kinematic detection.%
PCCN-Full retains 96.9\%: the physical impulse is absent regardless of when the
injection is timed, so PC-CWA produces near-zero PCS at all touch positions.%
The small performance loss relative to A0 (0.4 pp) reflects edge cases where the
resampled timing occasionally produces inter-event intervals close to the human lognormal
mean, slightly confusing the timing stream.%

\subsection{Overlay Agent Detection}
\label{sec:overlay}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_overlay}
  \caption{Overlay agent detection accuracy vs.\ number of server-selected challenge events.
  A single challenge event ($n{=}1$) achieves 91.3\% detection; passive mode cannot
  distinguish overlay agents from human sessions (61.4\%, near chance for this class).}
  \label{fig:overlay}
\end{figure}

A single challenge event achieves 91.3\% overlay detection accuracy (FPR 4.1\%),
rising to 97.8\% at $n{=}3$.%
The key mechanism is PCS localization: at $t^*$, the overlay agent produces an ADB
injection with PCS 0.07 (median), which falls below the OAL margin $\gamma{=}0.3$
and strongly activates the overlay class logit.%
PCCN-Passive achieves only 61.4\%---near chance for the overlay class---because
session-level $\bar{\mathrm{PCS}}$ is dominated by the genuine human touches,
making passive detection of this threat class impossible without active challenges.%

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_ablation}
  \caption{Ablation study (active mode, A0). Left: accuracy; right: EER.
  Removing PC-CWA causes the largest overall degradation; removing OAL collapses
  overlay-class F1 from 96.3\% to 71.4\%.}
  \label{fig:ablation}
\end{figure}

Removing PC-CWA (replacing with generic cross-modal attention) causes the largest drop
(3.9 pp ACC, 3.3 pp EER), confirming that physics-constrained attention is the dominant
discriminative component.%
Removing only the decay prior ($\tau_0$) while keeping the causal mask drops 2.2 pp,
showing that the exponential weighting provides independent benefit beyond the hard mask.%
Removing ISPM costs 2.6 pp overall but causes a disproportionate 18.9 pp drop in
ACC under A1 adversary specifically (not shown in the A0 table), consistent with
ISPM being the sole mechanism for motor-vs-finger spectral discrimination.%
Removing OAL has minimal effect on overall ACC (because overlay sessions are 12.5\%
of the dataset) but reduces overlay-class F1 from 96.3\% to 71.4\%---a 24.9 pp
degradation showing that OAL is essential for the overlay threat class.%
MAE pre-training contributes 3.2 pp, consistent with its role in cross-device
generalization.%

\subsection{Attention Visualization and Prototype Interpretability}
\label{sec:attention}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_attention}
  \caption{PC-CWA attention weights averaged over 480 human and 480 agent test sessions.
  Human sessions (left): attention self-organizes into the $[0,\,60]$\,ms causal window
  without explicit supervision on the window boundaries.
  Agent sessions (right): uniformly diffuse attention, reflecting the absence of any
  causal impulse structure.}
  \label{fig:attention}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig_proto}
  \caption{Learned ISPM spectral prototypes (no explicit spectral supervision).
  $\mathbf{p}_1$ captures the broadband energy of real finger impact (1--50\,Hz).
  $\mathbf{p}_2$ captures the narrowband oscillation of the ERM vibration motor ($\approx$18\,Hz).
  $\mathbf{p}_3$ captures the flat noise floor of software injection events.
  The prototypes independently recover the physics of the three impulse types.}
  \label{fig:proto}
\end{figure}

Fig.~\ref{fig:attention} shows that PC-CWA self-organizes attention into the $[0,\,60]$\,ms
post-touch causal window---narrower than the 80\,ms hard mask---without any supervision
on window boundaries.%
The learned $\tau_0$ converges to $38.2 \pm 4.7$\,ms across folds, consistent with
the physical impulse decay constant measured in Observation~1.%
Fig.~\ref{fig:proto} shows the three learned ISPM prototypes.%
Without any spectral annotation, $\mathbf{p}_1$ recovers broadband finger-impact energy,
$\mathbf{p}_2$ recovers narrowband motor oscillation at $\approx$18\,Hz (the driving
frequency of the ERM motor used in A1 sessions), and $\mathbf{p}_3$ recovers the flat
noise floor of agent injections.%
These results confirm that PCCN has rediscovered the underlying physics of the three
impulse types, providing a principled and interpretable explanation for every detection
decision.%
